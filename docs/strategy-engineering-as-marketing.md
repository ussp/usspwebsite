# Engineering as Marketing: Free Tools Strategy

> Source: [Starter Story interview with Bhanu](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Adl5_lJfkEE) — builder of SiteGPT ($13K MRR, $500K total revenue, $0 marketing spend)

---

## Core Concept

**Engineering as Marketing** (aka Free Tools Marketing / Side Project Marketing) — instead of spending on ads or doing sales outreach, build small free tools that:

1. Target keywords your potential customers are searching for
2. Rank organically on Google (SEO)
3. Include a CTA that funnels users to your main paid product

This is ideal for **builders who hate marketing** — you do what you're already good at (building) and let the tools do the marketing for you.

## The 7-Step Playbook

### Step 1: Discover All Keywords

Go to **Ahrefs Keywords Explorer** and leave the search field blank — then click search. This surfaces all keywords rather than limiting to what you *think* people search for.

### Step 2: Apply Include Filters

Add keyword filters relevant to your product. Examples:
- If building AI SaaS: include "AI"
- Add intent modifiers: "generator", "builder", "creator", "converter", "checker"
- Combine them: "AI generator", "AI builder", etc.

### Step 3: Filter by Keyword Difficulty (KD)

Set KD filter to **less than 10**. These are keywords any decent website can rank for without heavy SEO effort. Avoid high-difficulty keywords (e.g., "AI image generator" at KD 87).

### Step 4: Filter by Search Volume

Set minimum monthly search volume to **1,000+**. The whole point is driving traffic, so the keyword needs meaningful volume.

### Step 5: Build a Keyword List

Export to a Notion page (or spreadsheet) with columns:
- Keyword
- Monthly global search volume
- Keyword difficulty

### Step 6: Design CTAs for Each Tool

For each potential free tool, write a CTA that bridges to your main product. Example:

> "You just converted a PDF to chat. What if you could create a chatbot with ALL your business content? Try [Main Product]."

The tool should attract people who are **potential customers** of your paid product.

### Step 7: Prioritize and Build

Create a prioritization table with these columns:

| Column | What to Evaluate |
|--------|-----------------|
| Keyword | The target search term |
| Volume | Higher is better |
| KD | Lower is better |
| Build Effort | How long will it take to create? |
| Relevance | How closely does this tool's user match your ideal customer? |

**Prioritize:** High volume + Low KD + Easy to build + Highly relevant to main product.

Then build them, starting from the top of the list.

## Key Principles

### Relevance Over Volume
Every free tool must attract users who could become customers of your main product. A high-traffic tool with zero relevance is wasted effort.

### Compound Returns
Each new tool is a new SEO entry point. Over time, 50 tools create 50 ranking pages — the traffic compounds.

### AI Accelerates Everything
With existing tools as templates, you can prompt an AI coding tool: *"Look at these free tools. Create a new one in the same pattern for this keyword."* New tools in under 5 minutes.

### Low Ongoing Cost
Free tools are mostly static/simple — minimal hosting cost, no ad spend, no sales team. The traffic is organic and self-sustaining.

## Advice for Builders

> "Don't spend months and months on launching something. Launch with a very core feature of your product and let user feedback guide you to figure out what direction the product should go."

- **Launch fast** — SiteGPT went from idea to launch in 2 weeks, hit $10K MRR in the first month
- **Build what you'd use** — Bhanu built SiteGPT because his own customers needed it
- **Leverage what you're good at** — if you hate marketing but love building, this strategy turns building into marketing
- **Start with one tool, then scale** — once you have a template, each new tool is trivial to create
