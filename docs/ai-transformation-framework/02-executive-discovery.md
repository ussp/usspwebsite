# Chapter 2: Executive Discovery

## Purpose

Before building any framework details, we need to understand what matters to the client. Executive discovery is a structured listening process -- not a presentation. The goal is to learn the executive sponsor's priorities, concerns, and definition of success so the framework can be tailored accordingly.

## When This Happens

- **Phase:** Govern (early)
- **Timing:** First or second meeting with executive sponsor
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes
- **Who:** AI Transformation Leader + Engagement Director with executive sponsor

## The Meeting Dynamic

This is a **listening session**, not a pitch. The framework deck exists as a reference, not a slide-by-slide walkthrough.

**Structure:**

1. **Introduce the AI Transformation Leader** (5 min) -- background, what they bring, why they are here
2. **Ask the executive what they expect** (10 min) -- open-ended, let them set the agenda
3. **Show the framework as a draft** (15 min) -- "Here is what we have so far. It is not perfect. We want your feedback."
4. **Listen and capture feedback** (15 min) -- take notes, ask clarifying questions
5. **Agree on next steps** (5 min) -- what needs to happen before the pilot can begin

**Key principle:** The executive should talk more than you do.

## Discovery Questions

### Must-Ask (Top 5)

These questions should be covered in every executive discovery session, regardless of industry or engagement type:

1. **What does success look like to you?** -- Defines the north star for everything that follows
2. **What AI tools do we have access to right now?** -- Determines what is possible in the pilot
3. **What SDLC processes cause the most friction today?** -- Identifies where AI augmentation will have the most impact
4. **What concerns do you have about AI adoption?** -- Surfaces risks and resistance early
5. **Would a real-time dashboard showing pilot progress be useful, or do you prefer periodic reports?** -- Validates the leadership dashboard concept

### Good to Ask (If Time Permits)

6. What metrics do you currently track for team performance?
7. How do you define "quality" in the context of your delivery organization?
8. Are there teams you would recommend for the pilot? Why those teams?
9. What is the iteration cadence? When does the next cycle start?
10. Has an AI policy assessment been filed for the approved tools?

### Extended Discovery (Subsequent Sessions)

For larger engagements, a full discovery questionnaire (30+ questions across 9 sections) may be appropriate. Sections typically include:

- Vision and goals
- Current delivery performance
- Tool landscape
- Regulatory and compliance environment
- Team structure and culture
- Past change management experience
- Data and metrics maturity
- Risk tolerance
- Success criteria and reporting preferences

## After the Meeting

1. **Document** -- Write up meeting notes within 24 hours using the template above
2. **Share** -- Send notes to the executive for confirmation ("Did we capture this correctly?")
3. **Update the framework** -- Adjust the rollout plan, assessment priorities, and training focus based on what the executive said matters most
4. **Select KPIs** -- Use the executive's priorities to narrow from the full KPI catalog to the subset that will be tracked for this engagement
5. **Refine the deck** -- Update the presentation slides to reflect the executive's language and priorities

## Common Patterns

Based on experience across engagements, executives typically fall into recognizable patterns:

| What They Say | What It Means for the Framework |
|---------------|--------------------------------|
| "I want to see numbers" | Prioritize quantitative KPIs (velocity, cycle time, defect rate) |
| "I want my teams to be more efficient" | Focus training on time-saving SDLC processes (story writing, test generation) |
| "I don't want AI replacing anyone" | Emphasize "amplify, don't replace" messaging in all materials |
| "I've done this before" | Acknowledge their experience; adapt the framework to build on their past work |
| "I'm worried about compliance" | Front-load governance; make the compliance map a deliverable before the pilot |
| "Just show me it works" | Compress the pre-pilot phase; get to measurable results quickly |
