Step 1 · Facility Walk
Before any interviews, walk every zone of the facility and log what you observe. Don't ask questions yet — just observe and note.
💡 Walk first, talk second. What you observe before the interview is often more honest than what stakeholders tell you. Note what's actually happening vs. what they later say should be happening.
👁 What to Look For — Zone by Zone
For each area of the facility, note these 5 things:
1
Camera Coverage
Are there blind spots? Are cameras angled well? Count cameras per zone. Note height and angle.
2
Staff Behavior
Where are staff standing? Are they watching or distracted? Are any zones unmonitored?
3
Risk Zones
High-activity or restricted areas where incidents are most likely — e.g. entry/exit points, equipment areas, corridors, service zones. Note if they have dedicated monitoring.
4
Bottlenecks & Flows
Where do people queue, bunch up, or move unpredictably? Entry/exit chokepoints, mealtime flows, shift change patterns.
5
Existing Technology
What camera brand/model? Any NVR/DVR systems visible? Any access control panels, intercom, alarm systems?

Step 2 · Stakeholder Interview
Ask these structured questions to uncover pain points, compliance pressures, and what the client actually needs — vs. what they think they want.
💡 Listen for the pain, not the solution. If they say "we need AI cameras," ask why. The real answer is usually a safety event, audit failure, or staffing problem — that's your use case, not the camera.

Step 3 · Camera & Zone Audit
Map each camera to a zone and assess whether it can support AI inference. This determines what use cases are technically feasible and where.
💡 Not every camera is AI-ready. Poor angle, low resolution, severe backlighting, or fisheye lens can make a camera unusable for computer vision even if it works for human monitoring. Note these — they affect scope and cost.

Step 4 · Capture & Score Use Cases
Based on your walk, observations, and interview — define each use case and determine its model type, data needs, and development effort.
💡 Use the decision tree below for each use case before filling in the effort field. Answer the 4 questions in order — your effort level drops out automatically.
🌳 Effort Decision Tree — Run This for Every Use Case
Answer Q1 → Q2 → Q3 → Q4 in order. Stop as soon as you get a YES.
Q1 · Does a standard pre-trained model already detect this exact event out of the box?
✅ YES → Low effort. Configure zone polygons + thresholds only. No training needed.
❌ NO → Go to Q2
↓
Q2 · Does a standard model get you 60–80% there — just not on your specific environment or subject type?
✅ YES → Medium effort. Collect 50–200 real clips. Fine-tune existing model.
❌ NO → Go to Q3
↓
Q3 · Can you generate synthetic training data that realistically mimics the real event?
✅ YES → High effort — faster path. Synthetic (70%) + small real sample (30%).
❌ NO → High effort — data collection required. Must film real events.
↓
Q4 · Does this use case require tracking a person across time or across multiple cameras?
✅ YES → Very high effort. Full custom re-ID + tracking pipeline. Phase 2.
❌ NO → Single-frame detection. Effort stays at Q1–Q3 result.
