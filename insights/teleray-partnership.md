## USSP Scope: Selling TeleRay in Illinois Under TOPS

### Can TeleRay Be Sold Under TOPS?

TOPS Category Two covers **technology professional services** — not software licenses directly. But there is scope:

**What USSP can bill under TOPS:**
- Implementation services (deploying TeleRay at state facilities)
- EMR/EHR integration work (connecting TeleRay to existing hospital systems)
- Training and onboarding staff
- Configuration and customization
- Ongoing support and managed services
- Project management for rollouts across multiple facilities

**What likely needs separate procurement:**
- The TeleRay software subscription itself ($70-150/bed/month) — this is a SaaS product, not professional services
- However: many state agencies bundle SaaS + services into a single task order if the services component is significant
- Alternative: TeleRay subscription goes through a state software procurement vehicle (e.g., master contract, sole source under threshold), and USSP provides all the implementation services via TOPS

**Best approach:**
1. USSP wins the TOPS task order for implementation/integration services
2. TeleRay subscription is either bundled into the task order or procured separately by the agency
3. USSP earns revenue on both: margin on TeleRay resale + billable hours for implementation

## Valuation & Investment

**Current revenue:** ~$2M USD/year (ARR)
**Sites:** 3,000+ (primarily radiology)

### Revenue Breakdown (as of March 2026)

- **Radiology (Cloud PACS / imaging):** Majority of the $2M. Established product, 3,000 sites.

This means the $2M is concentrated in one product line. The virtual care side — which is the bigger growth opportunity — is still unproven revenue.

### Valuation Impact

| Scenario | Revenue Multiple | Valuation Range |
|---|---|---|
| Slow growth, single product concentration | 2-4x | $4M - $8M |
| Moderate growth, virtual care in early traction | 4-6x | $8M - $12M |
| Fast growth, virtual care scaling | 7-10x | $14M - $20M |

**Given that virtual care hasn't scaled yet, realistic valuation: $6M - $10M.**

**What pushes it higher:**
- Healthcare SaaS gets premium multiples (sticky, regulated, high switching costs)
- HIPAA compliant + FDA approved = barriers to entry
- 250+ EMR integrations = deep moat
- Per-bed recurring revenue model is attractive

**What holds it down:**
- Revenue concentrated in radiology — one product, one market
- Virtual care is still early deployment — the growth story is a bet, not a fact
- $2M ARR is still early stage
- Profitability, customer concentration, and churn rate unknown
