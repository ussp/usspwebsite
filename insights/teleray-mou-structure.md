# TeleRay MOU Structure

**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Pre-negotiation — use this to draft or guide legal counsel

---

## Recommended Approach: Reseller First, Investment in Parallel

**Start selling before investing.** Don't wait for due diligence to complete before generating revenue.

**Step 1: Sign a reseller agreement with IL government exclusivity now.**
- USSP starts selling TeleRay into IL state facilities immediately
- No investment commitment yet — just a letter of intent that both parties are exploring investment
- USSP needs: reseller agreement, wholesale pricing, demo environment access, 12-month minimum term

**Step 2: Run due diligence on investment in parallel.**
- Review financials, cap table, churn, revenue split, Axis partnership status
- Takes weeks/months to do properly — no reason to delay revenue while this happens

**Step 3: Close investment once due diligence is complete and USSP has real sales data.**
- By then, USSP has proven it can sell TeleRay in IL — stronger negotiating position
- USSP understands the product, the sales cycle, and the real demand — investment decision is informed, not speculative

**How to frame it to TeleRay:**
"We want to invest, and we're doing our due diligence. That takes time to do properly. But we don't want to wait — let's start with a reseller agreement for IL state government so we can begin selling immediately. We'll work the investment in parallel. If we close a few deals while the investment is being finalized, that's good for both of us."

**If TeleRay says "exclusivity only with investment":**
Push back: "Exclusivity is earned by selling, not by writing a check. Let us prove we can move your product in IL. That makes us both more confident in the investment."

**Why this is fair for both sides:**
- TeleRay gets a channel partner selling into a market they can't reach today — immediate revenue
- USSP gets to validate the opportunity before putting $500K+ at risk
- Successful early sales strengthen the case for investment on both sides
- Neither party is locked into something they can't back out of

---

## What the MOU Needs to Cover

The MOU has two separate relationships that should be clearly defined:

1. **Commercial partnership** (reseller + implementation)
2. **Investment** (equity stake + board seat)

These should be in the same MOU but clearly separated. The commercial terms should not be contingent on the investment closing, and vice versa — if one falls through, the other can still stand.

## Section 1: Reseller & Implementation Partnership

### Scope of Partnership

- USSP is authorized to resell TeleRay platform to end customers
- USSP is authorized to provide implementation, integration, and support services for TeleRay deployments
- Territory: start with Illinois state government, with option to expand

### Pricing & Margins

- TeleRay wholesale price to USSP: $70-80/bed/month (confirm exact number)
- USSP authorized to charge end customer up to $150/bed/month
- USSP retains the margin
- Pricing review: annually, with 90-day notice for any changes

### Exclusivity (Push For This)

- **Preferred:** USSP is exclusive reseller for Illinois state and local government accounts
- **Minimum acceptable:** USSP has right of first refusal on any IL government opportunity that comes to TeleRay directly
- **Protect yourself:** If TeleRay gets approached by an IL state agency, they route it to USSP before engaging directly or through another partner
- Non-compete: TeleRay will not appoint another reseller for IL government during the term

### Implementation Scope

- USSP handles: deployment, EMR/EHR integration, Axis camera setup/configuration, staff training, go-live support
- TeleRay provides: platform access, technical documentation, L2/L3 support escalation, API access for integrations
- Define SLA: TeleRay's response time for escalated support issues (e.g., 4-hour response for critical, 24-hour for non-critical)

### Sales & Marketing

- USSP can use TeleRay name, logo, and marketing materials in proposals to IL government
- Joint case studies from deployments (both parties must approve before publication)
- TeleRay provides demo environment for USSP to use in sales presentations

### Term & Termination

- Initial term: 3 years minimum (you're building a pipeline — don't let them pull the rug)
- Auto-renewal for 1-year periods unless 180-day written notice
- Termination for cause: material breach with 60-day cure period
- If terminated, USSP retains existing customer relationships and ongoing contracts through their natural term

## Section 2: Investment Terms

### Equity

- USSP's position: invest at $8-10M valuation (based on $2M ARR, radiology-concentrated revenue, virtual care not yet scaled)
- TeleRay's opening ask: $500K at $20M, stepping up to $35M across four tranches
- **Counter:** Single tranche of $500K at $8-10M valuation = 5-6% stake
- If TeleRay insists on higher valuation, tie subsequent tranches to milestones:
  - Tranche 2 unlocks only if virtual care reaches $500K ARR
  - Tranche 3 unlocks only if X new deployments or Y total beds live
  - No automatic step-ups without earned milestones

### Board Seat

- At 5%+ stake: request full board seat
- If they push back: accept board observer seat (attend meetings, see financials, no vote)
- Minimum: quarterly financial reporting and cap table transparency regardless of board seat

### Investment Structure

- Clarify: equity purchase, SAFE note, or convertible note?
- If SAFE/convertible: what's the cap? What's the discount?
- Pro-rata rights: USSP should have the right to participate in future rounds to maintain ownership percentage
- Anti-dilution: standard weighted-average anti-dilution protection

### Due Diligence (Before Signing)

- Full financials: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow (last 2-3 years)
- Revenue breakdown: radiology vs. virtual care (exact numbers)
- Customer list with contract values and renewal dates
- Churn rate: monthly and annual
- Cap table: all existing shareholders, options, SAFEs
- Any existing debt or liabilities
- Status of Axis partnership (terms, timeline)
- IP ownership: does TeleRay own all its technology, or are there licenses?
- Pending litigation or regulatory issues

---

## Section 3: Mutual Terms

### Confidentiality

- Standard mutual NDA covering all shared business information, customer data, pricing, and financials
- Survives termination for 2-3 years

### IP Ownership

- TeleRay retains all IP in the TeleRay platform
- USSP retains all IP in any implementation tools, processes, or integrations USSP develops
- Customer data belongs to the customer, not to either party

### Non-Solicitation

- Neither party hires the other's employees during the term + 12 months after
- Neither party directly solicits the other's customers for competing products during the term

### Governing Law

- Illinois (USSP's home state) — push for this
- Dispute resolution: mediation first, then arbitration (avoid litigation if possible)

## Negotiation Priorities (In Order)

1. **IL government exclusivity** — this is the most important commercial term. Without it, you build the pipeline and someone else takes the deals.
2. **3-year minimum term** — protects your investment in building the channel.
3. **Valuation at $8-10M** — reflects actual business stage, not aspirational.
4. **Milestone-based tranches** — if they want multiple investments, tie them to real progress.
5. **Board seat** — visibility into their decisions, financials, and roadmap.
6. **Existing customer protection on termination** — if the partnership ends, you keep the customers you brought in.
