## 4. Proposed Approach (Romi)

### Measurement & Baseline

The current velocity is the baseline. Specific metrics to agree on with client:

- **Story cycle time:** How long for a 5-point story from approval → pre-prod delivery?
- **Story authoring time:** How long for BA + PO + PM to produce a clear, approved story?
- **Test generation time:** How long for tester to write test scripts from acceptance criteria?
- **Story quality score:** Given defined criteria, does the story meet them? What's missing?

> "We can't just say 'hey, we're better.' We need a baseline so we can measure improvements."

### AI Use Cases Identified

| Role | AI Application |
|------|---------------|
| **BAs** | Generate acceptance criteria from user stories; validate story quality against criteria checklist; identify gaps |
| **Testers** | Generate starting test scripts from acceptance criteria (not perfect, but huge lift — testers refine) |
| **Developers** | Code assistance via GitHub Copilot |
| **Data team** | TBD (Chase to identify candidates) |

### Pilot-First Approach

- Jim agreed to a **pilot** approach
- **Duration:** 1 full PI (Program Increment = ~3 months)
- **Selection:** Subset of strong people across teams — strong BAs, developers, testers already identified
- **Weekly check-ins** during pilot: what's working, what's not, adjust
- **End of pilot:** gather results, produce role-based playbooks

### Training Before Rollout

- Structured training and direction before giving teams AI tools
- Not "here's Copilot, go knock yourself out"
- Effective prompting patterns and usage workflows per role

### Deliverable: Role-Based Playbooks

- BA Playbook
- Tester Playbook
- Developer Playbook
- Data Person Playbook
- Each with: best practices, prompts, workflows, what works / what doesn't

## 6. Early Lessons from Informal Testing

### John's Copilot Experiments with BAs

1. **Copilot as "spell checker" for stories:** Used Copilot to review existing stories for completeness
   - **Finding:** If the input story is poorly written, Copilot makes it *worse* — trims content, introduces sloppiness
   - **Red flag:** Less experienced BAs may not catch AI-introduced errors

2. **Copilot-only story generation (no human input):** Asked Copilot to write a story from scratch
   - **Finding:** Even messier results — pure AI generation without human guidance produces poor quality

3. **Key insight:** AI effectiveness is entirely dependent on the BA's skill to recognize when AI introduces problems

### John's Copilot vs Rovo Comparison

- Rovo produced **tighter user stories** than Copilot
- Likely because Rovo has access to Confluence context (project knowledge base)
- Copilot only knows what's in the code — without built-in documentation, it can't produce project-aware content
