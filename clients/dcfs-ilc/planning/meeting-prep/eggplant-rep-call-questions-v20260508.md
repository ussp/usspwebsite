# Eggplant Rep Call — Questions

**Purpose:** Establish what Eggplant AI we can use during the DCFS pilot (now through end of 2026), what licensing footprint DCFS already has vs. what additional AI tier would cost, and what vendor-side training is available so testers can ramp without burning project hours.

**Scope:** 30 minutes. Three buckets only — AI, licensing, training. Not a product demo.

---

## 1. AI capabilities

1. What AI features are GA in Eggplant today (May 2026)? Specifically:
   - Self-healing locators — GA, beta, or roadmap?
   - AI exploration on the digital twin — GA?
   - Keysight Generator (requirements → tests) — what version of Eggplant is it bundled with, and is it included or a separate SKU?
2. What is the public roadmap for late 2026 / 2027? We've heard a "vendor AI release end of 2026" referenced internally — can you confirm and name it?
3. Which of these features can be used in a governance envelope where **autonomous execution against production is forbidden**? (We need test-design and exploration assistance, not autonomous prod runs.)
4. Are there any public references in **state government or public-sector case-management contexts** we can review?

## 2. Licensing

1. **How many Eggplant licenses does DCFS currently hold?** Break down by product line (DAI / Functional / Performance / Generator) and by named vs. concurrent.
2. Which of those licenses include the AI capabilities above, and which would require an upgrade or add-on SKU?
3. If we want to enable Generator + self-healing for the pilot's tester cohort (~12 testers across 12 teams), what's the incremental cost and contracting path?
4. Is there a short-term pilot / evaluation license (30–90 day) we can use to validate the AI tier before committing to a full upgrade?
5. License model — per-named-user, concurrent, per-machine, or per-tenant?

## 3. Training plan

1. What vendor-provided training is available for the AI features specifically — self-paced, instructor-led, or both?
2. Estimated hours to ramp a tester from "uses Eggplant manually" to "productive with self-healing + Generator"?
3. Is there a **train-the-trainer** track? We'd prefer to certify Kamila + 1–2 leads who then run internal sessions, rather than vendor-leading every cohort.
4. Are training sessions included in the existing license, or billed separately?
5. Do you have role-specific curricula (tester, test lead, automation engineer), or is it one general track?
6. What's the typical cadence and lead time to schedule training — can we get a cohort started in May / early June?

---

## Outputs we want from the call

- A confirmed list of AI features GA today + a short roadmap statement we can quote in the pilot tool-authorization addendum.
- A current-license inventory + a pricing/contracting path for the AI tier.
- A training plan (curriculum + hours + cost + earliest start date) we can fold into the pilot training schedule.
