*(Point to the three cards)*

## Slide 6: Train, Pilot & Measure

**What's on screen:** Phase flow with Train/Pilot/Measure highlighted. Three columns with details and deliverables.

**What to say:**

*(Train column)* "**Training** is not generic AI training. Everyone gets a foundation module — DoIT compliance, data guardrails, what success looks like. Then it's role-specific: BAs learn to use AI for story quality and acceptance criteria. Testers learn test script generation. Developers learn code review and documentation with Copilot. Scrum Masters learn sprint insights and metrics interpretation. We also do a leadership briefing so you and the POs know what to expect and how to read the dashboard."

*(Pilot column)* "**Pilot** — 2 teams run with AI tools and training for one full PI. The remaining teams are the control group — same work, same conditions, no AI. This gives us a clean comparison. We do weekly check-ins to learn and iterate — this is not 'set and forget.'"

*(Measure column)* "**Measure** — pre and post comparison against baseline. Pilot teams versus control teams. We have 22 KPIs in our framework, but we'll select up to 8 that matter to you. Team health survey before and after. And at the end — lessons learned and a recommendation on whether to scale."

**If Jim asks "what KPIs?":** "We have a full catalog — 22 across velocity, quality, efficiency, team health, and migration categories. The appendix has the full list. But the ones we track should be driven by what matters to you — that's what we want to hear today."

**Transition:** "And then the final two phases — Playbook and Scale."

**Time:** ~3 minutes

## Slide 7: Playbook & Scale

**What's on screen:** Two columns — Playbook (4 role-based playbooks, what each includes) and Scale (go/no-go, AI champions, phased rollout, ART-wide measurement).

**What to say:**

*(Playbook column)* "**Playbooks** — four of them, one per role. BA, Tester, Developer, Scrum Master. Each one captures what works, what doesn't, the prompts that proved effective, guardrails, and lessons learned from the pilot. These are living documents — they start as drafts during Design, get tested during the Pilot, and are finalized based on real experience."

*(Scale column)* "**Scale** — if the pilot proves successful, we expand. But it's a phased rollout — not all 12 teams at once. The people who participated in the pilot become AI champions for the new teams. They coach, they share what worked, they help the next team ramp up faster. Measurement continues at the ART level."

"The end state we're going for: AI augmentation is just how teams work. Not a special project, not a temporary initiative — standard practice."

**Key message:** We're not asking Jim to commit to full scale today. We're asking him to let us pilot with 2 teams and prove it.

**Transition:** "That's what we've been putting together. Now the most important part — we want to hear from you."

## Slide 8: Discussion & Next Steps

**What's on screen:** Dark slide. "What we showed: Our intent." "What we need: Your expectations, priorities, and feedback." Four questions listed.

**What to say:**

"Jim, that's our intent — a framework we're building for ILC. It's not perfect and it's not finished. We showed it to you so you can shape it."

*(Pause. Let the questions on screen speak for themselves. Then ask them one at a time.)*

1. **"What does success look like to you?"** — This is the most important question. Listen carefully. Take notes. His answer determines which KPIs we track and how we define "done."

2. **"What AI tools do we have access to right now?"** — We know the State purchased Copilot and Rovo. But are they deployed? Can teams access them? Any blockers?

3. **"What should we focus on first?"** — Which SDLC processes cause the most friction? Where would AI make the biggest difference?

4. **"What concerns do you have?"** — Compliance? Quality? Team pushback? Tool readiness? Whatever he says, acknowledge it and explain how the framework addresses it.

**Do NOT rush this.** This is the meeting. Everything before this was setup. Let Jim talk. Take notes. Ask follow-up questions.

**Before wrapping up, confirm next steps:**
- "Based on what you've shared, here's what we'll do next: [adjust framework based on his input]"
- "We'll need [specific items he surfaced — JIRA access, tool deployment status, DoIT assessment]"
- "When you're back from travel, we'll have an updated plan ready for your review"
