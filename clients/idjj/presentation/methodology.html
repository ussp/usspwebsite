Measurement Design

Goal-Question-Metric (GQM) Paradigm

Basili, Caldiera & Rombach — University of Maryland / NASA, 1994
The canonical methodology for designing software measurement programs. Every metric must trace back to a business goal through an explicit question. We don't measure for measurement's sake.
Goal
Improve team productivity and quality by 5%+ via AI
Question
What metrics characterize that improvement?
Metric
Specific measurements selected per engagement
Basili, V. R., Caldiera, G., & Rombach, H. D. (1994). The Goal Question Metric Approach. In Encyclopedia of Software Engineering (pp. 528-532). Wiley.
Delivery Performance

DORA / Accelerate

Forsgren, Humble & Kim — Google DevOps Research & Assessment, 2018
Four key metrics validated across 23,000+ respondents, 2,000+ organizations over 7+ years. The definitive research linking engineering practices to organizational performance.
Metric Dimension Direction What It Measures
Deployment Frequency Throughput Higher = better How often the team releases to production
Change Lead Time Throughput Lower = better Time from code commit to running in production
Change Failure Rate Stability Lower = better % of deployments causing failures/rollbacks
Mean Time to Recovery Stability Lower = better Time to restore service after an incident
Key finding: Speed and stability are correlated, not conflicting. High performers excel at both. Teams that deploy more frequently also have lower failure rates.
Forsgren, N., Humble, J., & Kim, G. (2018). Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps. IT Revolution Press.
Team Productivity

SPACE Framework

Forsgren, Storey et al. — Microsoft Research / ACM, 2021
Five dimensions of developer productivity. No single metric is sufficient — always measure at least 3 dimensions spanning at least 2 SPACE categories to prevent gaming.
Dimension What It Captures Example Metrics
Satisfaction How fulfilled the team feels Job satisfaction survey, tool satisfaction
Performance Outcomes and impact of work Sprint predictability, quality scores
Activity Count of actions (use carefully) PRs, commits, stories completed
Communication How information flows Requirement clarity, review turnaround
Efficiency Minimal waste and delays Cycle time, flow state time, context switches
Rule: Always measure at least 3 dimensions spanning at least 2 SPACE categories. This prevents gaming and gives a holistic view of team health.
Forsgren, N., Storey, M.-A., et al. (2021). The SPACE of Developer Productivity. ACM Queue, 19(1), 20-48.
Story Quality

Quality User Story (QUS) Framework

Lucassen et al. — Utrecht University, 2016
13 criteria across 3 dimensions for evaluating user story quality. Validated on 1,023 stories from 18 companies. Companion tool AQUSA automates quality defect detection using NLP.
Syntactic
Well-formed, Atomic, Minimal
Semantic
Conceptually Sound, Problem-oriented, Unambiguous, Conflict-free
Pragmatic
Full Sentence, Estimatable, Unique, Uniform, Independent, Complete
Lucassen, G., Dalpiaz, F., et al. (2016). Improving agile requirements: the Quality User Story framework and tool. Requirements Engineering, 21(3), 383-403.
Before/After Measurement

Six Sigma DMAIC

Harry & Schroeder, 2000
The standard before/after measurement methodology. Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control — explicitly designed for proving process improvements with statistical rigor.
Metric Definition How We Apply It
First Pass Yield Stories passing QA on first attempt / total Story quality improvement measurement
Rework Rate Items requiring rework / total delivered Waste measurement — AI should reduce this
Cost of Poor Quality Hours on rework × blended rate Budget impact calculation for executives
DPMO Defects per million opportunities Process capability benchmark
Harry, M. J., & Schroeder, R. (2000). Six Sigma: The Breakthrough Management Strategy. Currency/Doubleday.
International Standard

ISO/IEC 25010:2023 — SQuaRE

International Organization for Standardization, 2023
The international standard defining 8 quality characteristics and 31 sub-characteristics for software product quality. Gold standard for government engagements.
CharacteristicRelevance
Functional SuitabilityCompleteness, Correctness
ReliabilityMaturity, Fault tolerance
MaintainabilityModularity, Testability
SecurityConfidentiality, Integrity
Measurement formulas (ISO 25023):
Functional Correctness = 1 − (Incorrect results / Total results)
Modifiability = 1 − (Modifications causing degradation / Total modifications)
ISO/IEC. (2023). ISO/IEC 25010:2023 Systems and software engineering — SQuaRE — Product quality model.

Industry Research

Capers Jones — Defect Removal Efficiency

Applied Software Measurement, 3rd ed., 2008
DRE = Defects found before release / Total defects (including 90-day post-release). U.S. average: ~85%. Best-in-class: >99%.
Key insight: Testing alone is <50% efficient at finding defects. Combined inspections + static analysis + testing: >95%. AI-assisted reviews can close this gap.
Jones, C. (2008). Applied Software Measurement: Global Analysis of Productivity and Quality. 3rd ed. McGraw-Hill.
