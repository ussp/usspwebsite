## 1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to explore a potential business collaboration in connection with:

> The design, deployment, operation, and maintenance of AIS 140 compliant Vehicle Location Tracking Device ("**VLTD**") systems and Monitoring Centres for Indian state transport authorities, under the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways ("MoRTH") Nirbhaya Framework scheme
> (the "**Purpose**").

In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose to the other Confidential Information, and this Agreement governs the treatment of that information.

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**"Confidential Information"** means any non-public information disclosed by one Party ("**Disclosing Party**") to the other ("**Receiving Party**"), in any form (oral, written, electronic, visual), whether or not marked as confidential, including but not limited to:

(a) technical information — product specifications, source code, architectures, algorithms, designs, device Type Approval Certificates, test data, roadmaps;
(b) commercial information — pricing, margins, customer lists, supplier lists, OEM partnerships, revenue data, financial statements;
(c) strategic information — bid strategy, pursuit plans, government counterparty interactions, competitive positioning;
(d) operational information — processes, methodologies, staffing plans, training materials;
(e) third-party information — information received from customers, partners, or government entities that the Disclosing Party is obliged to keep confidential;
(f) any summaries, notes, analyses, or derivatives prepared by the Receiving Party that incorporate Confidential Information.

**"Representatives"** means a Party's directors, officers, employees, consultants, legal and financial advisors, and affiliated companies, in each case who have a need to know the Confidential Information for the Purpose.

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party shall:

3.1 Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and no other purpose whatsoever;

3.2 Protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar sensitivity, and in any case no less than a reasonable standard of care;

3.3 Disclose the Confidential Information only to its Representatives who (a) need to know it for the Purpose, (b) have been informed of its confidential nature, and (c) are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement;

3.4 Be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives;

3.5 Not copy, reproduce, reverse-engineer, decompile, or analyse the Confidential Information except to the extent necessary for the Purpose;

3.6 Not use the Confidential Information for its own competitive benefit or to the disadvantage of the Disclosing Party;

3.7 Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorised use or disclosure of which it becomes aware.

## 4. EXCLUSIONS

The obligations in Clause 3 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate through contemporaneous written records:

(a) was in the public domain at the time of disclosure, or subsequently entered the public domain through no fault of the Receiving Party;
(b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession before disclosure by the Disclosing Party;
(c) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to any Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party;
(d) was lawfully received from a third party not under a confidentiality obligation to the Disclosing Party.

## 5. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES UNDER LEGAL COMPULSION

If the Receiving Party is required to disclose Confidential Information by law, court order, or the order of a competent regulatory authority, it shall:

5.1 Give the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (to the extent legally permitted) before making the disclosure, so that the Disclosing Party may seek protective measures;
5.2 Cooperate with the Disclosing Party's reasonable efforts to limit or contest the disclosure;
5.3 Disclose only the minimum information legally required.

## 6. NO GRANT OF RIGHTS

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any ownership, licence, or other right in the Confidential Information or in any intellectual property of the Disclosing Party, other than the limited right to use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose during the term of this Agreement.

## 7. TERM AND DURATION

7.1 This Agreement shall come into effect on the date first written above (the "**Effective Date**") and shall continue for a period of **three (3) years**, unless earlier terminated by either Party by thirty (30) days' written notice.

7.2 Notwithstanding termination, the confidentiality obligations under Clauses 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, and 9 shall survive and continue to bind the Receiving Party for a period of **five (5) years** from the date of each disclosure.

## 8. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon written request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall, within thirty (30) days:

8.1 Return all Confidential Information in tangible form, including all copies, extracts, and derivatives;
8.2 Permanently delete all Confidential Information in electronic form (subject to reasonable archival retention in secure backup systems, which shall remain subject to this Agreement);
8.3 Certify such return and destruction in writing, signed by an authorised officer.

## 9. REMEDIES

The Parties acknowledge that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled, without prejudice to any other remedy available at law or in equity, to seek **injunctive relief** and **specific performance** from a court of competent jurisdiction, in addition to recovery of actual damages, costs, and reasonable legal fees.

## 10. NO OBLIGATION TO TRANSACT

Nothing in this Agreement obliges either Party to enter into any further agreement, transaction, or commercial relationship. Any binding commercial commitment shall require a separate, written, duly executed agreement.

## 11. NO REPRESENTATION OR WARRANTY

Confidential Information is provided "as is". Neither Party makes any representation or warranty, express or implied, as to the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for purpose of any Confidential Information disclosed.

## 12. NO ASSIGNMENT

Neither Party shall assign, transfer, or sub-license its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that either Party may assign this Agreement to an affiliate under common control or to a successor in connection with a merger, reorganisation, or sale of substantially all of its business.

## 14. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

14.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

14.2 Any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, including its validity, breach, termination, or interpretation, shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, as amended.

14.3 The seat and venue of arbitration shall be **Hyderabad, Telangana, India**. The language of arbitration shall be English. The arbitral tribunal shall consist of a **sole arbitrator** mutually appointed by the Parties, or failing agreement, appointed in accordance with the Act.

14.4 Subject to Clause 14.2, the courts at Hyderabad shall have exclusive jurisdiction for any interim or injunctive relief.

## 15. MISCELLANEOUS

15.1 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties regarding Confidential Information and supersedes all prior written or oral understandings on the subject.

15.2 **Amendment.** Any amendment to this Agreement shall be in writing, signed by authorised representatives of both Parties.

15.3 **Severability.** If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force.

15.4 **Waiver.** No waiver shall be effective unless in writing, and no single or partial waiver shall constitute a waiver of any subsequent breach.

15.5 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including by electronic / scanned signatures, each of which shall constitute an original.

15.6 **Stamping.** This Agreement shall be stamped in accordance with the Indian Stamp Act as applicable in the State of Telangana.

15.7 **Relationship.** Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship between the Parties.
