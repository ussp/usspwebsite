## RECITALS

**WHEREAS** the Parties are exploring a joint pursuit of AIS 140 VLTD and Monitoring Centre opportunities in India, beginning with the State of Andhra Pradesh, as more particularly described in the Memorandum of Understanding dated **[MOU DATE]** ("**MOU**");

**WHEREAS** in the course of such collaboration, each Party will introduce the other to, or grant the other access to, certain valuable counterparties, contacts, customers, suppliers, and opportunities which constitute proprietary business relationships;

**WHEREAS** each Party wishes to ensure that the other shall not exploit such introductions, contacts, or knowledge to its own advantage or to the disadvantage of the introducing Party;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**"Introduced Party"** means any government, customer, supplier, OEM, advisor, or other third party that is first introduced, disclosed, or identified by one Party ("**Introducing Party**") to the other Party ("**Receiving Party**") in connection with the Purpose, including without limitation:

(a) **Government counterparties introduced by USSPPL** — including but not limited to the Andhra Pradesh Department of Transport, the Office of the Hon'ble Minister for Transport (Andhra Pradesh), the Commissionerate of Transport (Andhra Pradesh), Andhra Pradesh Police, Andhra Pradesh Emergency Response Support System (ERSS / Dial-112), Regional Transport Offices (RTOs) in Andhra Pradesh, and any individual officers, officials, or advisors at such bodies with whom USSPPL facilitates contact;
(b) **OEM and technology partners introduced by Fleetronix** — including AIS 140 TAC-certified VLTD OEMs, device manufacturers, connectivity providers, map service providers, and industry advisors;
(c) **Customer and commercial contacts** — any enterprise, government, or public sector entity introduced by either Party.

**"Opportunity"** means any present or future business transaction, project, tender, bid, procurement, partnership, or commercial arrangement involving an Introduced Party, including specifically the AIS 140 VLTD and Monitoring Centre schemes under the MoRTH Nirbhaya Framework.

**"Purpose"** has the meaning given in the MOU (the joint pursuit of AIS 140 VLTD and Monitoring Centre engagements).

**"Term"** has the meaning given in Clause 7.

## 2. NON-CIRCUMVENTION

### 2.1 Undertaking
During the Term, neither Party shall, directly or indirectly, and whether alone or in association with any third party:

(a) Contact, communicate with, solicit, or transact with any Introduced Party of the other Party for any Opportunity, except through or with the written consent of the Introducing Party;
(b) Use any information regarding an Introduced Party obtained through the other Party to pursue an Opportunity independently;
(c) Interfere with, undermine, disrupt, or seek to influence the relationship between the Introducing Party and its Introduced Parties;
(d) Introduce, refer, or recommend any Introduced Party to a third party without the prior written consent of the Introducing Party, where doing so would enable that third party to pursue an Opportunity that could otherwise accrue to the Introducing Party;
(e) Enter into any direct contractual, commercial, employment, advisory, or consultancy relationship with an Introduced Party of the other Party, in respect of any Opportunity, without the Introducing Party's prior written consent.

### 2.2 Application to USSPPL Government Contacts (Explicit)
Without limiting Clause 2.1, Fleetronix expressly undertakes that it shall **not**, during the Term:

(a) Approach, directly or through any representative, agent, intermediary, or third party, the Office of the Hon'ble Minister for Transport of Andhra Pradesh, or any officer or official of the Andhra Pradesh Department of Transport, regarding any VLTD, AIS 140, Monitoring Centre, Nirbhaya Framework, or related opportunity, except in the name of and under the authority of USSPPL as prime contractor under the MOU;
(b) Participate in, contribute to, support, or advise any third-party bid, proposal, or pursuit for the Andhra Pradesh AIS 140 VLTD and Monitoring Centre scheme;
(c) Disclose to any third party the content, timing, or existence of USSPPL's engagements with Andhra Pradesh government counterparties.

### 2.3 Application to Fleetronix OEM and Technology Partners (Explicit)
Without limiting Clause 2.1, USSPPL expressly undertakes that it shall **not**, during the Term:

(a) Directly contact, solicit, or transact with any OEM, technology partner, connectivity provider, or map service provider that Fleetronix has introduced or recommended to USSPPL, in respect of the Purpose, except through Fleetronix;
(b) Use Fleetronix's OEM partnership structure, negotiated pricing, or commercial terms to procure equivalent devices or services independently of Fleetronix.

## 3. NON-COMPETE (LIMITED AND MUTUAL)

### 3.1 Scope
During the Term, with respect to the **State of Andhra Pradesh** specifically, and the **AIS 140 VLTD and Monitoring Centre scheme under the MoRTH Nirbhaya Framework**, neither Party shall, directly or indirectly:

(a) Submit a competing bid, proposal, or tender response;
(b) Partner with, advise, or support any third-party bidder;
(c) Acquire or take a commercial interest in any competing entity that is bidding for the Andhra Pradesh scheme.

### 3.2 Scope Limitation
This non-compete is expressly limited to the Andhra Pradesh AIS 140 VLTD and Monitoring Centre scheme. It does **not** restrict either Party from:

(a) Pursuing AIS 140 opportunities in other Indian states (subject to Clause 4.2 of the MOU);
(b) Conducting its existing business in other sectors, states, or customer segments;
(c) Fulfilling commitments under contracts existing as of the Effective Date.

### 3.3 Enforceability
The Parties acknowledge that Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, restricts general non-compete covenants. The Parties agree that this Clause 3 is limited in scope, geography, and duration, and is entered into to protect legitimate business interests in a specific collaborative pursuit, and is therefore enforceable as a reasonable and narrow restriction. **[Counsel to confirm enforceability in view of Indian case law.]**

## 4. NON-SOLICITATION

### 4.1 Employees and Consultants
During the Term and for a period of **eighteen (18) months** thereafter, neither Party shall, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, or employ any employee, director, officer, or consultant of the other Party who has been involved in the Purpose, without the other Party's prior written consent.

### 4.2 Exceptions
Clause 4.1 shall not apply to:

(a) General public advertisements or recruitment campaigns not specifically targeting the other Party's personnel;
(b) Responses by the other Party's personnel to such general advertisements, provided no direct solicitation occurred;
(c) Personnel whose employment with the other Party has ended at least six (6) months prior to contact.

### 4.3 Customers and OEMs
During the Term, neither Party shall solicit customers or OEMs of the other Party that have been introduced under the Purpose, except in accordance with Clause 2 (Non-Circumvention).

## 5. BREACH AND REMEDIES

### 5.1 Acknowledgement of Harm
The Parties acknowledge that breach of this Agreement, particularly Clauses 2 and 3, would cause significant, immediate, and potentially irreparable harm to the non-breaching Party, including loss of the very opportunity forming the subject of the Parties' collaboration.

### 5.2 Liquidated Damages
Without prejudice to any other remedy, the Parties agree that in the event of a material breach of Clause 2 (Non-Circumvention) or Clause 3 (Non-Compete), the breaching Party shall pay the non-breaching Party liquidated damages equal to:

**Three (3) times the estimated value of the Opportunity that has been circumvented or competed for in breach**,

such amount being a genuine pre-estimate of loss and not a penalty. The "estimated value" shall be determined by reference to the scheme value published by MoRTH, the bid value, or the contract value awarded, whichever is highest.

### 5.3 Injunctive Relief
The non-breaching Party shall additionally be entitled to seek, without posting bond and without prejudice to any other remedy, **injunctive relief, specific performance, and such other equitable remedies** as a court of competent jurisdiction may grant.

### 5.4 Costs and Legal Fees
The breaching Party shall bear all costs and reasonable legal fees incurred by the non-breaching Party in enforcing this Agreement.

## 6. CARVE-OUTS

### 6.1 Pre-Existing Relationships
The obligations in this Agreement shall not apply to relationships, contacts, or engagements that a Party can demonstrate through written records existed prior to the Effective Date of this Agreement. Each Party shall disclose such pre-existing relationships in a **Schedule of Pre-Existing Engagements** to be exchanged within fourteen (14) days of the Effective Date.

### 6.2 Publicly Available Contacts
Contact with persons whose professional roles are publicly disclosed (e.g., published government officials, public procurement officers acting through tender portals) shall not be deemed a breach, **provided that** such contact is not in the context of an Opportunity covered by this Agreement.

### 6.3 Government Mandated Engagement
Nothing in this Agreement shall prevent either Party from complying with a legally mandated disclosure, summons, or government request.

## 7. TERM

### 7.1 Commencement
This Agreement shall come into effect on the date first written above (the "**Effective Date**").

### 7.2 Duration
This Agreement shall continue in force for a period of **thirty-six (36) months** from the Effective Date, or until **twelve (12) months** after the conclusion or termination of the Initial Project under the MOU (including its 2-year O&M period), whichever is later.

### 7.3 Survival
Clauses 4.1 (Non-Solicitation of Employees), 5 (Breach and Remedies), 8 (Governing Law), and 9 (Miscellaneous) shall survive termination for the durations specified or in perpetuity as applicable.

## 8. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

8.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

8.2 Disputes shall be resolved by arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

8.3 **Seat and venue:** Hyderabad, Telangana. **Language:** English. **Tribunal:** sole arbitrator, mutually appointed.

8.4 Notwithstanding the above, the Parties expressly agree that the courts at **Hyderabad** shall have exclusive jurisdiction to grant **interim injunctive relief**, and each Party consents to the jurisdiction of those courts for such purpose.

## 9. MISCELLANEOUS

9.1 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the MOU and NDA, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties on the subject matter.

9.2 **Amendment.** In writing, signed by authorised representatives.

9.3 **Severability.** If any provision is held invalid, the remainder shall continue; the invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable while preserving its intent.

9.4 **Waiver.** No waiver except in writing. Partial waiver does not waive subsequent breaches.

9.5 **Assignment.** No assignment without prior written consent, except to an affiliate under common control.

9.6 **Counterparts.** Electronic signatures permitted.

9.7 **Stamping.** As per Telangana Stamp Act.

9.8 **No Partnership.** Nothing creates a partnership, agency, or employment relationship.
