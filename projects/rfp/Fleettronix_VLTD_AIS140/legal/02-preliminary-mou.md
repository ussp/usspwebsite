## RECITALS

**WHEREAS** USSPPL is a technology services company with over twenty (20) years of delivery experience, an Indian operating entity based in Hyderabad, and parent delivery capability through US Software Professionals Inc., USA;

**WHEREAS** Fleetronix is a Hyderabad-based telematics and fleet intelligence technology company, founded and led by an all-woman leadership team, with 7,000+ deployed devices, 4.5 Lakh+ trips onboarded, and Fortune 500 customer references;

**WHEREAS** the Government of India, Ministry of Road Transport and Highways ("**MoRTH**"), has issued the Motor Vehicles (Vehicle Location Tracking Device and Emergency Button) Order, 2018 (Gazette S.O. 5453(E)) and the Scheme Letter dated 13 January 2020 (RT-16011/1/2018-T) under the Nirbhaya Framework, funding States and Union Territories to establish AIS 140 compliant Monitoring Centres for public service vehicle tracking;

**WHEREAS** the Parties intend to jointly pursue and deliver AIS 140 compliant VLTD and Monitoring Centre engagements for Indian state transport authorities, beginning with the State of Andhra Pradesh;

**WHEREAS** the Parties wish to record their preliminary understanding on roles, scope, commercial framework, and exclusivity, pending execution of a definitive Teaming Agreement;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. PURPOSE

The Parties agree to collaborate on the pursuit, bid preparation, proposal submission, and (if awarded) delivery of AIS 140 compliant VLTD and Monitoring Centre projects for Indian state transport authorities, with initial focus on the **State of Andhra Pradesh** ("**Initial Project**").

## 2. ROLES

### 2.1 USSPPL — Prime Contractor

USSPPL shall act as the **prime contractor** and sole contracting entity with the government counterparty. USSPPL's responsibilities shall include:

(a) Government and regulatory interface, including with the state Transport Department, RTO, ERSS / Dial-112, and all statutory authorities;
(b) Bid management, including submission of EMD (Earnest Money Deposit), PBG (Performance Bank Guarantee), and other financial instruments;
(c) Contract negotiation and execution with the government counterparty;
(d) Monitoring Centre software, dashboard, and operator platform;
(e) Integration layers — VAHAN, ERSS / Dial-112, NIC Cloud, Survey of India-compliant mapping;
(f) Physical Monitoring Centre build, 24x7 operations, and staffing;
(g) Tier 1 and Tier 2 support — helpdesk (Telugu / English / Hindi), software, and integration escalations;
(h) Training programs for RTO, MVI, AMVI, and permit holders;
(i) CERT-IN security audit engagement;
(j) Prime contractor legal, compliance, insurance, and regulatory obligations.

### 2.2 Fleetronix — Technology Partner

Fleetronix shall act as the **technology and field partner**. Its responsibilities shall include:

(a) Selection and recommendation of AIS 140 Type Approval Certified ("**TAC-Certified**") VLTD hardware, through partnership with certified Original Equipment Manufacturers ("**OEMs**") where Fleetronix does not itself hold a TAC;
(b) Device procurement, logistics, inventory, and distribution;
(c) Device fitment at RTOs and fitment centres;
(d) Field installation in vehicles;
(e) Hardware annual maintenance, repair, and replacement;
(f) SIM and M2M connectivity provisioning on the device;
(g) Tier 3 hardware support and escalation to the OEM;
(h) Contribution of content, imagery, and capability information for joint marketing materials and bid submissions.

### 2.3 Shared Responsibilities

Certain activities shall be jointly executed with a named lead, including:

(a) System architecture design — Fleetronix lead, USSPPL technical review;
(b) Capability pitch deck and bid response — USSPPL lead, Fleetronix technical content;
(c) Field device commissioning and go-live — USSPPL logistics lead, Fleetronix technical lead;
(d) Incident response — USSPPL customer-facing, Fleetronix technical triage.

## 3. INDICATIVE COMMERCIAL FRAMEWORK

The following is a non-binding indicative framework. Binding commercial terms shall be recorded in the definitive Teaming Agreement (Clause 10).

| Item | Indicative Position |
|---|---|
| Prime Contractor | USSPPL |
| Revenue / Cost share — project phase | **USSPPL 60% : Fleetronix 40%**, subject to final scope split verification |
| Revenue / Cost share — O&M (2-year) | Same ratio as project phase |
| Device cost | Pass-through at actual cost from Fleetronix to USSPPL; Fleetronix retains margin on device versus OEM |
| Payment flow | Government pays USSPPL; USSPPL pays Fleetronix on a back-to-back milestone basis |
| EMD / PBG | USSPPL arranges; Fleetronix contributes pro-rata to its revenue share |
| Change orders | Priced and shared in the same ratio as base scope |

## 4. EXCLUSIVITY

### 4.1 Mutual Exclusivity for Initial Project (BINDING)

For the duration of this MOU and any definitive Teaming Agreement that follows, neither Party shall:

(a) Pursue the Initial Project (State of Andhra Pradesh AIS 140 VLTD and Monitoring Centre opportunity) independently or with any third party;
(b) Submit a competing bid, directly or indirectly, for the Initial Project;
(c) Partner with any direct competitor of the other Party for the Initial Project.

### 4.2 Other States and Opportunities

For AIS 140 VLTD and Monitoring Centre opportunities in Indian states **other than Andhra Pradesh**, the Parties shall discuss in good faith whether to pursue jointly, on a case-by-case basis. Neither Party is obliged to partner exclusively with the other for other states.

### 4.3 Carve-Outs

Pre-existing customer relationships and commercial engagements of each Party, as of the Effective Date, are not restricted by this MOU. The Parties shall exchange a written schedule of such pre-existing engagements within fourteen (14) days of the Effective Date.

All Confidential Information exchanged under or in connection with this MOU shall be treated in accordance with the NDA, the terms of which are incorporated herein by reference.

## 6. NON-CIRCUMVENTION (BINDING)

The Parties acknowledge that they have executed, or shall execute contemporaneously, a Non-Circumvention Agreement dated **[DATE OF NCA]** ("**NCA**"). Each Party's obligations under the NCA are incorporated herein by reference and shall apply to all government counterparties, customer contacts, and OEM partners introduced by the other Party in connection with this MOU.

## 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 7.1 Pre-Existing IP
Each Party shall retain all right, title, and interest in its pre-existing intellectual property. Nothing in this MOU transfers or licenses any pre-existing IP, except to the limited extent necessary to perform activities under this MOU.

### 7.2 New IP — Project Customisation
Intellectual property created specifically for the Initial Project, whether by USSPPL, Fleetronix, or jointly, shall be owned as follows (to be finalised in the Teaming Agreement):

(a) **USSPPL-developed MC software, dashboards, and integration layers** — owned by USSPPL, with a perpetual, royalty-free licence to Fleetronix solely for purposes of supporting the Initial Project;
(b) **Fleetronix-developed platform adaptations** — owned by Fleetronix, with a perpetual, royalty-free licence to USSPPL solely for purposes of operating the Initial Project delivery and its 2-year O&M;
(c) **Jointly developed work product** — jointly owned, with each Party having full use rights.

### 7.3 Deliverables to Government
Deliverables provided to the government counterparty shall carry only those IP licenses required by the government contract. No transfer of underlying source IP shall occur unless expressly required.

## 8. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

Each Party represents and warrants that:

(a) It is duly incorporated and in good standing under applicable law;
(b) It has full authority to enter into this MOU;
(c) Execution and performance of this MOU does not breach any contract or obligation to a third party;
(d) It is not debarred, blacklisted, or under investigation by any Indian central or state government procurement body.

## 9. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 9.1 Term
This MOU shall come into effect on the date first written above (the "**Effective Date**") and shall continue until the earlier of:

(a) Execution of a definitive Teaming Agreement between the Parties;
(b) Award of the Initial Project to a third party or cancellation of the Initial Project tender;
(c) Twelve (12) months from the Effective Date, unless extended by mutual written agreement.

### 9.2 Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate this MOU immediately by written notice upon:

(a) Material breach by the other Party not cured within thirty (30) days of written notice;
(b) Insolvency, winding-up, or similar proceedings against the other Party;
(c) Change of control of the other Party without prior written consent.

### 9.3 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:
(a) The binding provisions of this MOU (Clauses 4.1, 5, 6, 7, 11, and 12) shall survive in accordance with their terms;
(b) The NDA and NCA shall continue in force in accordance with their own terms;
(c) Non-binding commercial provisions shall lapse.

## 10. PATH TO DEFINITIVE AGREEMENT

The Parties shall work in good faith to execute a **definitive Teaming Agreement** within **thirty (30) days** of the issuance of the formal RFP / tender for the Initial Project, or within such other timeframe as mutually agreed. The Teaming Agreement shall record binding commercial terms, including final revenue share, SLA definitions, indemnities, liability caps, insurance, and dispute resolution.

## 11. NON-BINDING NATURE (CLARIFICATION)

Except for the binding provisions expressly identified (Clauses 4.1, 5, 6, 7, 11, 12), this MOU is a statement of intent and does **not** create binding commercial obligations. No claim for breach of non-binding provisions may be made against either Party.

## 12. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION (BINDING)

12.1 This MOU shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

12.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this MOU shall be resolved by arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

12.3 Seat and venue: **Hyderabad, Telangana**. Language: English. Sole arbitrator, mutually appointed.

12.4 Courts at Hyderabad shall have exclusive jurisdiction for interim relief.
